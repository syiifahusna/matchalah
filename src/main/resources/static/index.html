Fun Facts About Green Tea

Ancient Origins

Green tea originated in China over 5,000 years ago and was initially used as medicine before becoming a popular beverage.

Same Plant, Different Process

All tea varieties (green, black, white, oolong) come from the same plant, Camellia sinensis. The difference is in how the leaves are processed!

Catechin Power

Green tea contains powerful antioxidants called catechins, which may boost metabolism and help protect against various diseases.

Popular Green Tea Varieties

Matcha

Matcha is a finely ground powder of specially grown and processed green tea leaves. It contains higher amounts of caffeine and antioxidants than regular green tea because you consume the entire leaf.

It's traditionally used in Japanese tea ceremonies and has become popular worldwide in lattes, desserts, and more.

Sencha

Sencha is the most popular green tea in Japan, accounting for about 80% of tea produced there. It has a refreshing taste with sweet, grassy, and slightly bitter notes.

The leaves are steamed to prevent oxidation, then rolled, shaped, and dried, resulting in thin, needle-like tea leaves that brew a clear, yellow-green tea.

Did You Know?

Green tea can boost brain function

Green tea contains caffeine and L-theanine, which can improve brain function, mood, and alertness.

Second largest consumed beverage

After water, tea is the second most consumed beverage in the world.

Green tea ice cream

In Japan, green tea (matcha) is a popular ice cream flavor and appears in many desserts!
